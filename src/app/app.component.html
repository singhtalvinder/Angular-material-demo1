Khalsa (Punjabi: ਖ਼ਾਲਸਾ, [ˈxaːlsaː], "the sovereign") refers to both a special group of initiated Sikhs,
as well as a community that considers Sikhism as its faith. The Khalsa tradition was initiated in 1699
by the last living Guru of Sikhism, Guru Gobind Singh. Its formation was a key event in the history of
Sikhism.(src: wikipedia)
